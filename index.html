Timeline of Kazi Nazrul Islam's Life:

  • 1899 - Born in Churulia, West Bengal, India

  • 1923 - Imprisoned by British authorities for sedition due to his revolutionary writings
  • 1924 - Marries Pramila Devi while continuing his work in literature and music
  • 1930 - Joins the Indian National Congress and continues to advocate for independence
  • 1933 - Writes his famous novel "Mrityu-Kshuda" (Hunger and Death)
  • 1941 - Composes the song "Karar Oi Louho Kopat" which becomes a rallying cry for freedom fighters
  • 1942 - Suffers from a mysterious illness that gradually silences his voice
  • 1952 - Awarded the Padma Bhushan by the Government of India
  • 1972 - Moves to Bangladesh and is declared the National Poet of Bangladesh
  • 1976 - Passes away in Dhaka, Bangladesh

Nazrul's influence on Bengali culture and his relentless spirit of rebellion have left an indelible mark. His poetry, music, and activism continue to inspire millions across the world.
